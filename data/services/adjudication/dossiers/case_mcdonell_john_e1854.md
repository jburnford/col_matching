> McDONELL, JOHN.—Clk. in the imigrn. office of N. S. Wales in 1854; promoted in 1856 sen. clk. to the regisr.-gen.; placed in charge of the compilation of the gen. statistics of the col. in 1858; sec. of the pol. dept. of Queensland on its separation from N.S.W., Feb., 1860; imigrn. agt., Jan., 1866, inspr. of benevolent asylum and orphanages, also visiting justice to H.M.'s gaol and lun. asyl.; as well as ch. inspr. of distilleries; under-sec. and permanent head of the post and telegraph dept. in 1870; major-comdt. of the Queensland volr. forces, 1877; lieut.-col. comdt. in 1878; ret., Nov., 1879, and placed on the staff with his rank as lieut.-col.; ag. comdt. of the defence forces in Feb., 1883, to 1884; mem. of imigrn. bd. since 1865.
