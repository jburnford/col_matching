> HETHERSETT, ANDREW LANIONU.—Ed. C.M.S. Coll. Inst., Abeokuta; out-door officer, Lagos, May, 1871; harbour master's clerk, 1871; clerk and interpreter to a mission, under Mr. Goldsworthy, to Ondo, in April, 1872; headman, harbour police, 1873; second clerk and interpreter, administrator's office, 1876; chief clerk and interpreter, 1879. On special mission to Abeokuta, 1881; on special mission of condolence under Capt. Douglas to Ikorodu after attack on it by Jebu Remo refugees, 1884; accompanied Capt. Denton as interpr. on mission to Jebu Ode, 1891, and Sir G. Carter in 1892 (medal and clasps), and accompanied latter on mission, Jan. to April, 1893, to put an end to the tribal wars in Jebu; chief clk. and interpr., Aborigines Dept., Aug., 1895.

**Version `col1883-L27979.v` — printed in editions [1883, 1886]:**

> HETHERSETT, ANDREW LANIONU.—Entered colonial government service, as outdoor officer, in May, 1871; harbour master's clerk, 8th May, 1871; detached and served as clerk and interpreter to a mission, under Mr. Goldworthy, to open up the Ondo Road to the east of Lagos for commerce and other political purposes, and to bring home to his kingdom from exile Ode Ondo, the Ondo king, in April, 1872; headman, harbour police, 1873; harbour master's clerk, 1st May, 1875; second clerk and interpreter, administrator's office, 1st Jan., 1876; first clerk and interpreter in the same office, 1st Jan., 1879.
