> SULLIVAN, W. W., Q.C.—Attorney-general and premier of Prince Edward Island, Canada; born in P.E.I., 1849; called to the bar in 1867; created a Q.C. by the government of Prince Edward Island in 1876, and by the governor-general of Canada under letters patent in 1879; is a deputy judge in the admiralty court, president of the board of education, and president of the board of justices of P.E.I. Hospital for the Insane; has been a member of the executive council, holding the office of solicitor-general, from April, 1873, till Sept., 1876, when he resigned; was appointed leader of the opposition in the legislature in 1877; became prime minister and attorney and advocate-general in 1879, which position she continues to hold.
