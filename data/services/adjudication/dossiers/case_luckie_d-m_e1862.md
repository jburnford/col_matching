> LUCKIE, D.M., F.S.S.—Trained in a lawyer's office in Montrose, Scotland; in 1862 emigrated to N. Zealand, as editor and part proprietor of the "Nelson Colonist," which he largely popularised; in 1872 was elected member of parliament for Nelson City; in 1878 became editor of the "Southern Cross;" and when it was amalgamated with the "New Zealand Herald," the leading journal in the colony, became chief editor of the combined papers; in 1878 accepted the post of Government insurance commissioner.
