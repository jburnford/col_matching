> GALIZIA, Hon. E. LEWIS, C.E. and Architect, Member Inst. C.E.—Draughtsman, public works department, Malta, 1846; in 1852 received warrant to practise the profession of surveyor and architect; assistant surveyor and valuator of landed property, land department, July, 1854; government surveyor in 1856, and member of the university board for examination of candidates for the profession of surveyors and architects; chief government surveyor in 1860; in 1861 agent of the Malta government in connection with the works for the extension of the Great Harbour, carried out by the Admiralty through contractors. Complete survey and a set of record and descriptive plans of all government property in Malta and Gozo were made under his direction in 1859-66. In 1878 and 1879 proceeded on two special missions to Cyprus for reporting on the island generally, and also as to its convenience for a Maltese settlement; April, 1880, consulting engineer to sanitary medical board; June, 1880, was appointed director of public works, with a seat in the council of government ex officio; president of the local commission for the Colonial and Indian Exhibition, 1886.
