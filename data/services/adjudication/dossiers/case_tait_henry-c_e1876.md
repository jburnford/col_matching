> TAIT, HENRY C.—British vice-consul at Ambrosia, S.W. Africa, 20th Feb., 1876; acting consul for the Bights of Benin and Biafra and the Island of Fernando Po, West Coast of Africa, 11th July, 1877; accompanied the Niger expedition with H.M.S. "Pioneer," "Avon," and "Boxer," in Aug., 1877; to punish the perpetrators of outrages on the S.S. "Sultan of Sokotoo;" negotiated treaties with the king and chiefs of Osumari and Ouitha, Upper Niger; was sent on a special mission with presents from Her Majesty's government to the Emir of Nupe, and King of Bida, and Sultan of Sokotoo; made various treaties with the kings and chiefs of Old Calabar, Cameroons, &c.; is a corresponding member of the Zoological Society of London; left the West Coast of Africa, owing to ill health, 1878; entered the civil service of Natal in April, 1881; third clerk customs department, 24th March, 1883.

**Version `col1883-L29686.v` — printed in editions [1883]:**

> TAIT, Henry C.—British vice-consul-at Ambriz, S.W.Africa, 20th Feb., 1876; acting consul for the Bights of Benin and Biafra and the Island of Fernando Po, West Coast of Africa, 11th July, 1877; accompanied the Niger expedition with H.M.S. "Pioneer," "Avon," and "Boxer," in Aug., 1877, to punish the perpetrators of outrages on the S.S. "Sultan of Sockatoa;" was present at the bombardment of Emblama and Kfama, river Niger, and negotiated treaties with the king and chiefs of Osumari and Onitsha, Upper Niger; received the thanks of Commodore Sir F. Sullivan for services and active co-operation during the expedition, and favourably mentioned in despatches to the Lords of the Admiralty; received the approval of treaties and action taken regarding the Niger from the right hon. the Secretary of State for Foreign Affairs; was sent on a special mission with presents from Her Majesty's government to the Emir of Nupe, and King of Bida, and Sultan of Sockatoa; made various treaties with the kings and chiefs of Old Calabar, Cameroons, &c.; is a corresponding member of the Zoological Society of London; left the West Coast of Africa, owing to ill health, in 1878; entered the civil service of Natal in April, 1881; confirmed in appointment as second clerk, resident magistrate's office, Durban.
