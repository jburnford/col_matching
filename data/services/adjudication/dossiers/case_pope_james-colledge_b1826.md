> POPE, HON. JAMES COLLEDGE.—Born at Bedeque, Prince Edward Island, 11th June, 1826; entered the legislative assembly of that colony, 1857, and continued to hold a seat in that body since then (except during a few months in 1873, when he sat in the Dominion House of Commons) until 1876, when defeated upon the school question; entered the executive council in 1859; was premier from 1865 to 1868, and from 1870 to 1872; and in 1873 was called upon to form a new administration, which he succeeded in doing. The construction of the Prince Edward Island Railway and the successful negotiation of the "better terms" on the colony entering the Dominion as a province, were two of the achievements of his government. Returned for Prince County, Prince Edward Island, to Canadian House of Commons, November, 1876; again returned at last general election, and sworn a member of the Queen's Privy Council for Canada, 1878; minister of marine and fisheries, 1878; resigned, 1881.
