> HORN, ARTHUR EDWIN, C.M.G. (1923), M.D., B.Sc. (Lond.); M.R.C.P. (Lond.); D.T.M. and H. (Cantab.); certif., Lond. Sch. of Trop. Med. (with distinctions).—Ed. at St. Mary's Hosp. and London Univ.; civil surg., 19th brigade field hosp., S. African field force, 1900-1901; apptd. to W. African medical staff, 1904 and has served in G. Coast, N. and S. Nigeria and Gambia; awarded Craggs research prize for original research in trop. med. (London S.T.M.), 1908; rec'd thanks of S. of S. for the Colonies for report on cerebro-spinal fever in Northern Territories of the G. Coast, 1908, and for report on sleeping sickness in the Volta River dist. of the G. Coast, 1910; seconded for special service at the C.O., 1910-12; personal asst. to the P.M.O., S. Nigeria, 1913; senr. med. offr., ad mem., exec. and leg. couns., Gambia, 1914-16; prin. civ. med. offr., German E. Africa, 1917-19; attd. Rockefeller comm. for investigation of yellow fever in W. Africa, 1920; dir., med. and sanitary services, Malaya, 1921-22; pres. elect for 5th Congress of Far Eastern Assoc. of Trop. Med., 1923; med. sec. to Col. Advisory Med. and Sanitary Comttee. at C.O., 1923-24; junr. med. adviser to C.O., 1924; consulting physician to C.O., 1928; mem., exec. comttee. of Br. Empire Leprosy Relief Assoc.; fellow R. Soc'y. of Med.; R. Soc'y. Trop. Med.; author of "Health of Europeans in W. Africa," "Health of Europeans in E. Africa."
