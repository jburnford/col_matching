> JOLY DE LOTBINIERE, SIR HENRI GUSTAVE, K.C.M.G. (1895).—Born in France, Dec. 5th, 1829. Educated in Paris, and called to the bar of Quebec in 1853. Elected to parliament of old Canada for Lotbiniere in 1861, and continued to represent the county till 1867, when he was returned both to the parliament of Dominion and to the Legislature of Quebec. In 1874 he retired from parliament to devote his attention to provincial affairs. In 1878, on dismissal of the De Boucherville cabinet, he was called on to form a ministry, which he did, holding power for one session by virtue of the vote of the speaker; he resigned 1879, after defeat in house of assembly; later he retired from leadership of the party; and in 1886, as a protest against the Riel agitation carried on by the provincial liberal party, retired from public life. In 1877 was offered the portfolio of agriculture in Mackenzie government, with a seat in the senate, controller of inland revenue, without a seat in the cabinet, in Mr. Laurier's govt., June, 1896.
