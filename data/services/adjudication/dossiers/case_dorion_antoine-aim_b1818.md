> DORION, HON. SIR ANTOINE AIMÉ, KNT. BACHELOT (creat. 1877).—Born 1818; called to the bar of L. C., 1842; created a Q.C. 1868; was commissioner of crown lands, Canada, from 2nd to 4th August, 1858; provincial secretary from May, 1862, to January, 1868; attorney-general of L. C. from May, 1863, to March, 1864; and minister of justice of the dominion from 7th November, 1873, to 1st June, 1874, when appointed chief justice of the Court of Queen's Bench for the Province of Quebec, in the Dominion of Canada; was administrator of the government of the Province of Quebec for some weeks in 1876.
