> McARTHUR, JAMES JOSEPH.—B. 1836; asst. on stintl. surveys in Manitoba, 1872-3; prov. land survr., 1878; Dom. land survr., 1879; engaged until 1886 in stintl. meridian and base line surveys in N.W. Territories, and expln. of N. Sask. and Brazzau rvs.; photo-topographic survey of the Rocky Mts., 1881-92; photo-topographic surveys along the coast of S.E. Alaska, prelim. to stintl. of boundary dispute, 1893; mapped region from Portland Canal to Mt. St. Elias; conducted explor. expds. through the Yukon country, 1897-9; on staff of high joint comm., Washington, D.C., as topographic expert and geographer, 1899; engrnr. to boundary comm. appd. to lay down provisional boundary at head of Lynn Canal, 1900; in conjunction with U.S. engrns., recovered the boundary line between Br. Columbia and the State of Washington, 1901; attached to staff of Br. agt. before Alaskan boundary tribunal at F.O., as geographer, 1903; engrnr. to Br. commr., and in charge of final demarcation, 1904-11; asst. internat. boundary commr., 1909; has conducted extensive topographical surveys throughout Canada; H.M. commr. to determine, in conjunction with a U.S. commr., the boundaries between Canada and the U.S., according to existing treaties, 1916; pres., Assoc. of Dom. Land Survrs., 1917; chmn., Ottawa centre, Roy. Astron. Soc. of Can.; fellow, Roy. Astron. Soc. of Can., 1918; mem., Engng. Inst. of Can.
